# SECTION 2: COLD CALLING MASTERY

## 2.1 The Psychology of the First 10 Seconds

**CRITICAL INSIGHT:** Prospects decide within 5-10 seconds whether to continue listening or hang up. 82% will disengage by the 30-second mark if they don't hear something relevant.

### The Prospect's Mental Process

```
SECOND 0-3:   "Who is this?" (Identification scan)
SECOND 3-7:   "Why are they calling ME?" (Relevance check)
SECOND 7-10:  "Is this worth my time?" (Value assessment)
SECOND 10+:   Decision: Continue or Terminate
```

### What Triggers Hang-Ups
1. **"How are you today?"** - Screams "sales call"
2. **Rushed/scripted delivery** - Signals telemarketer
3. **Overly enthusiastic tone** - Feels fake
4. **Generic company pitch** - Not relevant to them
5. **Pause after pickup** - Indicates auto-dialer

### What Captures Attention
1. **Pattern interrupts** - "This is a cold call, feel free to hang up..."
2. **Immediate local relevance** - "...homes in [their neighborhood]..."
3. **Curiosity hooks** - "...did you know [surprising fact]?"
4. **Pain acknowledgment** - "...seeing Duke Energy rates jump..."
5. **Calm, confident tone** - Signals authority

## 2.2 Admiral Energy Opening Framework

### The PERMISSION-BASED OPENER (Primary)

```
"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with Admiral Energy in [City], NC. 
I'm not calling to pitch you‚ÄîI just saw you grabbed our Solar Savings Checklist 
and wanted to make sure it landed in your inbox. Did you get a chance to look at it?"
```

**Why it works:**
- Immediately identifies who + where (trust signal)
- States what you're NOT doing (lowers defense)
- References their action (relevance)
- Asks a simple yes/no question (engagement)

### The CURIOSITY OPENER (For Cold Lists)

```
"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with Admiral Energy. I'm calling because 
homeowners in [City/County] are seeing their Duke Energy rates jump this year‚Äî
have you looked into the new 2025 federal tax credits that can offset that?"
```

**Why it works:**
- Local relevance (Duke Energy + their area)
- Pain point (rate jumps)
- Curiosity trigger (new tax credits)
- Question invites dialogue

### The PATTERN INTERRUPT OPENER (For Skeptical Markets)

```
"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name]‚ÄîI know this is a cold call, 
so feel free to hang up. But if you've got 20 seconds, I can tell you 
why [X] homeowners on your street went solar last year. Fair enough?"
```

**Why it works:**
- Disarms by acknowledging the interruption
- Gives them control ("feel free to hang up")
- Creates curiosity (neighbors)
- Asks for specific micro-commitment (20 seconds)

## 2.4 Cold Calling Methods: Admiral Standards

### Method 1: PRECISION TARGETING (Primary)

**NOT "Smile and Dial"‚ÄîPrecision and Intent**

| Traditional Approach | Admiral Approach |
|---------------------|------------------|
| 200 random calls/day | 80-120 targeted calls/day |
| Generic pitch | Personalized 3x3 prep |
| Volume = success | Quality conversations = success |
| Burn through lists | Nurture and qualify lists |

**The 3x3 Prep Rule:**
Before every call, spend 3 minutes finding 3 key insights:
1. **Location context** (neighborhood, utility, HOA status)
2. **Prior interaction** (website visit, content download, referral)
3. **Relevant trigger** (EV owner, high bill area, recent outage zone)

### Method 2: MULTI-CHANNEL SEQUENCING

Never call in isolation. Follow this cadence integration:

```
Day 0: Lead magnet download ‚Üí Immediate call (Speed-to-Lead)
Day 1: No contact (let them read)
Day 2: Email 2 + Value Call (afternoon)
Day 3: Social touch (LinkedIn view)
Day 5: Email 3 (Case Study)
Day 6: Power Hour Call (Tues 10-11am)
Day 8: Email 4 (Urgency + SMS opt-in push)
Day 9: Break-Up Call
Day 12: Final Email
Day 14: Recycle to long-term nurture
```

### Method 3: CURIOSITY-DRIVEN DIALOGUE

**Never pitch on the first call.** The goal is to earn the right to a longer conversation.

| DON'T | DO |
|-------|-----|
| "Let me tell you about our solar solutions" | "What have you heard about solar in NC?" |
| "We're the leading installer in the state" | "Have you noticed your Duke bills going up?" |
| "I can save you thousands of dollars" | "What would you do with an extra $150/month?" |

### Method 4: OBJECTION EXPLORATION (Not Battle)

**Never "overcome" objections‚Äîexplore them.**

When prospect says: **"I'm not interested"**

‚ùå Wrong: "But you haven't heard what we can do for you..."

‚úÖ Right: "Totally understand. Just curious‚Äîis it the timing, or have you looked into solar before and something turned you off?"

When prospect says: **"It's too expensive"**

‚ùå Wrong: "Actually, it's very affordable with our financing..."

‚úÖ Right: "That's a common concern. When you say expensive, are you thinking about the upfront cost or the monthly payment? Because those are very different conversations."

# SECTION 3: TCPA COMPLIANCE TRAINING

## 3.1 Federal Compliance Requirements

### The Non-Negotiables (Zero Tolerance)

| Rule | Requirement | Penalty |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Call Timing** | 8:00 AM - 9:00 PM LOCAL TIME of prospect | $500-$1,500/violation |
| **DNC Compliance** | Scrub lists against National DNC every 31 days | Up to $50,120/call |
| **Consent for Autodialers** | Prior Express Written Consent required for automated dialing to cell phones | $500-$1,500/violation |
| **Identification** | Must identify yourself and company immediately | FTC/State AG action |
| **Internal DNC** | Must honor opt-out requests within 30 days max | $500-$1,500/violation |
| **Call Frequency** | Max 3 calls per 24 hours in FL, MD, OK | State penalties |

### Understanding Consent Levels

| Consent Type | Required For | How to Obtain |
|--------------|--------------|---------------|
| **Prior Express Written Consent (PEWC)** | Autodialer calls/texts to cell phones for marketing | Signed form, website checkbox with clear disclosure |
| **Prior Express Consent (PEC)** | Informational automated calls | Customer provides number |
| **No Consent Needed** | Manual dial to non-DNC landlines during legal hours | N/A |

## 3.2 North Carolina Specific Rules

### NC Telemarketing Requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **State License** | Required to telemarket to NC residents |
| **Call Hours** | 8:00 AM - 9:00 PM (matches federal) |
| **State DNC** | NC uses National DNC Registry (no separate list) |
| **Robocall Restriction** | Prerecorded messages require consent or live operator introduction |
| **Disclosure** | Must identify company immediately; no misrepresentation |
| **B2B Exemption** | Business-to-business calls generally exempt from NC telemarketing statute |

### NC Penalties
- 1st offense: $500
- 2nd offense: $1,000
- 3rd+ offense: $5,000 each
- Consumer can sue for $500/violation (treble for willful)

## 3.3 Illegal Calling Scenarios (NEVER DO THESE)

### ‚ùå PROHIBITED ACTIONS

1. **Calling DNC-listed numbers** without documented consent or EBR
2. **Calling outside 8am-9pm** in prospect's LOCAL time zone
3. **Calling on Sundays** in: AL, LA, MS, SD, UT (and holidays in PA, RI)
4. **Using autodialers to cell phones** without Prior Express Written Consent
5. **Texting without opt-in** (texts = calls under TCPA)
6. **Calling after opt-out** - once they say "don't call," STOP FOREVER
7. **Calling same number 4+ times in 24 hours** (FL, MD, OK = max 3)
8. **Spoofing caller ID** or failing to display real callback number
9. **Claiming government affiliation** ("I'm from the solar program...")
10. **Lying about who you are** or purpose of call

## 3.5 When a Prospect Says "Take Me Off Your List"

### Immediate Actions (Within the Call):
1. **Acknowledge:** "Absolutely, I'm removing you right now."
2. **Confirm:** "Can you confirm this number is [read it back]?"
3. **Apologize:** "I apologize for the interruption. You won't hear from us again."
4. **Log Immediately:** Flag record as DNC in CRM BEFORE hanging up

### Post-Call Actions:
1. Record date, time, and rep name of opt-out request
2. Sync to Internal DNC list across ALL systems
3. Never call, text, or email this number again for marketing
4. If they call back later wanting info, document the new consent

# SECTION 4: THE VALUE OF CADENCES OVER CALL SPAMMING

## 4.1 The Problem with "Spray and Pray"

### What Happens When You Spam Calls:

| Effect | Consequence |
|--------|-------------|
| **Number gets flagged as "Spam Likely"** | iOS silently sends to voicemail; Android shows warning |
| **Connect rates plummet** | 24-32% of new numbers pre-flagged by carriers |
| **Prospects associate you with spam** | Brand damage before you even speak |
| **Burns through leads** | Calling someone 10x who never answers = wasted opportunity |
| **Rep burnout** | Endless rejection without strategy leads to 15-month average tenure |
| **Compliance risk** | Excessive calling can trigger harassment claims |

### The Data on Multi-Touch Cadences

| Approach | Call-to-Meeting Rate |
|----------|---------------------|
| Random cold calling | ~2% |
| Precision targeting alone | ~4% |
| Precision + Multi-channel cadence | **5-7%** |
| Precision + Multi-channel + Insight-driven | **6.7%+** |

---

## 4.2 Why Cadences Win: The Psychology

### Single Call Problem:
- Prospect doesn't know you ‚Üí High distrust
- No context for why you're calling ‚Üí Immediate dismissal
- One chance to make impression ‚Üí Pressure leads to mistakes
- No follow-up ‚Üí Interested prospects lost

### Cadence Solution:
- **Email 1:** Plants seed, provides value (lead magnet)
- **Call 1:** References email ‚Üí Familiarity established
- **Email 2:** Reinforces value ‚Üí Credibility building
- **Call 2:** Different time ‚Üí Catches them when available
- **Social Touch:** Shows you're a real person ‚Üí Humanizes outreach
- **Email 3:** Social proof ‚Üí Neighbors/testimonials reduce risk
- **Call 3:** By now they recognize your number/name ‚Üí Warmer reception

### Each Touch Adds:
```
Touch 1: Recognition (They've heard of you)
Touch 2: Consideration (They're thinking about you)
Touch 3: Familiarity (They know what you offer)
Touch 4: Trust (They've seen proof)
Touch 5: Readiness (They're prepared to discuss)
```
