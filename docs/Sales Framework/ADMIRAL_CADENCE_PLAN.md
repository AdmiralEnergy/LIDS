## 2.2 Day-by-Day Execution Guide

### DAY 0: SPEED-TO-LEAD

**Actions:**
1. Send Email 1 (Lead Magnet Delivery)
2. Call within 5-10 minutes of download

**Goal:** Verify receipt of guide + human connection

**Call Script (Permission-Based):**
```
"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with Admiral Energy in [City], NC. 
I saw you grabbed our Solar Savings Checklistâ€”I'm not calling to pitch you, 
just wanted to make sure it landed in your inbox okay?"
```

**If No Answer - Voicemail:**
```
"Hey [Name], this is [Your Name] from Admiral Energy. 
Just wanted to make sure you got the solar guide you downloaded. 
I'll send a quick email with the link again just in case. 
No need to call back unless you have questions. Take care!"
```

**Why Speed Matters:**
- Leads contacted within 5 minutes are 21x more likely to convert
- After 30 minutes, response rates drop 100x

---

### DAY 1: NO CONTACT

**Action:** Nothing

**Why:** Avoids "desperate telemarketer" perception. Let them read and absorb the guide. Shows you're not pushingâ€”you're helping.

---

### DAY 2: VALUE CALL + EMAIL

**Actions:**
1. Send Email 2 (Key Benefits/Win)
2. Call in afternoon (different time block from Day 0)

**Goal:** Reference the email to bridge the gap

**Call Script (Curiosity Hook):**
```
"Hi [Name], I sent over a tip this morning about the new federal tax credits. 
It got me thinkingâ€”did you know [Their County] has specific incentives 
that stack on top of the federal credit?"
```

**NC-Specific Hooks:**
- Duke Energy's PowerPair program
- Net metering policy changes
- Local utility rate increases

**If They Engage:**
Ask discovery questions:
- "What's your average monthly Duke Energy bill?"
- "Have you experienced any outages in the last year?"
- "Have you looked into solar before, or is this new territory?"

### DAY 3: SOCIAL TOUCH

**Action:** View their LinkedIn profile (if available)

**Why:** This is a "soft touch" that shows research without being intrusive. Many people see LinkedIn profile views. It signals:
- You're a real person
- You did some homework
- You're not just a call center bot

**Optional Expansion:**
- Light engagement on public social posts
- Do NOT send connection request yet (too early)

---

### DAY 5: SOCIAL PROOF EMAIL

**Action:** Send Email 3 (Case Study)

**Content Guidelines:**
- Feature a local NC homeowner (preferably nearby county/city)
- Show specific numbers (savings amount, bill reduction %)
- Include a quote/testimonial
- End with soft CTA: "Curious if you'd see similar results? Reply and I'll run a quick analysis for your home."

**Why This Works:**
- 87% of consumers say reputation matters in solar decisions
- Local social proof builds trust (peer validation)
- Testimonials work better than company claims

---

### DAY 6: POWER HOUR CALL

**Timing:** Tuesday, 10-11 AM (data shows higher connect rates)

**Goal:** Overcome "Trust Deficit"

**Call Script (Differentiation):**
```
"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from Admiral Energy. 
I know a lot of folks in NC are skeptical because of all those 
'free solar' robocalls going around. 

I wanted to clarifyâ€”we're a local [City] installer, veteran-owned,

I sent yesterday about your neighbor's results?"
```

**Key Points to Hit:**
- Acknowledge the trust problem directly
- Differentiate: local, veteran-owned, not a call center
- Reference the case study (continuity from Day 5)
- Ask if they've reviewed the material

**If They Push Back:**
```
"I totally get itâ€”there's a lot of noise out there. 
What would help you feel more comfortable? Would it help 
if I just showed you the math for your specific home, 
no pressure, just information?"
```

### DAY 8: URGENCY EMAIL + SMS PUSH

**Action:** Send Email 4 (Urgency + SMS Opt-in CTA)

**Critical Step:** This email pushes the "Text SOLAR to [Code]" call-to-action

**Email Elements:**
1. Light urgency (incentive deadline, utility rate changes)
2. Quick-win offer (free analysis, no-commitment consultation)
3. SMS opt-in CTA: "Text SOLAR to [Your Number] for instant quote"

**Why SMS:**
- SMS has 98% open rate vs. 20% for email
- Text consent = explicit TCPA opt-in for future texts
- Creates direct, immediate communication channel

---

### DAY 9: BREAK-UP CALL

**Goal:** Elicit a "No" or a "Yes"â€”remove the "Maybe"

**Call Script (Negative Reverse):**
```
"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from Admiral Energy. 
I haven't heard back from you, so I'm going to assume 
solar isn't a priority for your home right now?"
```

**Psychology:** People fight to correct you if they are interested. This approach:
- Gives them an easy out if genuinely uninterested
- Triggers response from those who ARE interested but busy
- Removes limbo leads from active pipeline

**If They Say "No, Actually I Am Interested":**
```
"Greatâ€”I didn't want to assume. What's been holding you back 
from scheduling a quick call to see the numbers? 
I can do 15 minutes anytime this week."
```

**If They Confirm Not Interested:**
```
"No problem at all. Mind if I askâ€”is it the timing, 
or did you have a concern about solar in general? 
I ask because it helps us understand what homeowners 
are really looking for."
```
(Capture the insight for market intelligence)

---

### DAY 12: FINAL NUDGE EMAIL

**Action:** Send Email 5 (Multiple Channel Reminder)

**Content:**
- Brief recap of what you've sent
- Restate the value proposition
- Offer multiple response options: email, call, text, website
- Clear "last chance" framing without being pushy

**Subject Line Examples:**
- "Quick question before I close your file"
- "Should I follow up next quarter instead?"
- "One last thing about [Their County] solar incentives"

---

### DAY 14: RECYCLE OR NURTURE

**Decision Point:**

| Outcome | Action |
|---------|--------|
| **Appointment Booked** | Move to Closer pipeline |
| **Expressed Future Interest** | Schedule specific follow-up date |
| **No Response, No Opt-Out** | Long-Term Nurture (1 email/month) |
| **Opted Out** | Mark DNC, remove from all lists |

**Long-Term Nurture:**
- 1 email per month maximum
- Value-focused content (no hard sells)
- Seasonal triggers (summer = high bill season, winter = battery backup)
- Do NOT continue calling (preserves phone number reputation)

# SECTION 3: CRITICAL EXECUTION RULES

## 3.1 Do's and Don'ts

| Category | DO | DON'T |
|----------|-----|-------|
| **Phone Numbers** | Rotate Caller IDs across calls | Use same number all day (triggers spam flags) |
| **Opening Line** | Be direct about who you are | Say "How are you today?" (sales tell) |
| **NC Context** | Mention storms/outages (grid anxiety) | Ignore local relevance |
| **Tone** | Low & slow (signals authority) | Rush or sound hyper (signals desperation) |
| **TCPA** | Manual dials unless explicit opt-in | Auto-dial without written consent |
| **Frequency** | Max 3 attempts per lead in 24 hours | Call 5-10 times in one day |

## 3.2 The NC Curiosity Hook

```

are seeing their Duke Energy rates jump this year, 
and I wanted to see if you've looked into the new 
2025 tax credits that can help offset that?"
```

**Why This Works:**
- Local relevance (Duke Energy + their area) âœ“
- Pain point (rate jumps) âœ“
- Curiosity trigger (new tax credits) âœ“
- Question invites dialogue âœ“
- All in under 15 seconds âœ“

# SECTION 4: CADENCE INTELLIGENCE LOOP

## 4.1 What to Capture on Every Call

| Data Point | Where to Log | How to Use |
|------------|--------------|------------|
| **Objection Type** | CRM disposition + notes | Update FAQ, refine scripts |
| **Questions Asked** | CRM notes | Create content addressing them |
| **Competitor Mentions** | CRM field: Competitor | Build competitive intelligence |
| **Incentive Confusion** | CRM field: Confusion Point | Clarify marketing messaging |
| **Best Time Reached** | CRM: Time Connected | Optimize dial scheduling |
| **Decision Timeline** | CRM: Follow-up Date | Plan re-engagement |

## 4.2 Weekly Cadence Review Questions

After each week, analyze:

1. **Which emails got the most replies/clicks?**
   - Double down on winning subject lines/content
   
2. **Which call day/time had best connect rate?**
   - Adjust Power Hour timing if needed
   
3. **What objection came up most often?**
   - Update talk track to preempt it
   
4. **Where did prospects drop off?**
   - Strengthen that touch point
   
5. **What touch finally got the meeting?**
   - Ensure that touch is never skipped

## 4.3 A/B Testing Calendar

| Week | Test Element | Variation A | Variation B |
|------|--------------|-------------|-------------|
| 1 | Email 1 Subject | "Your Solar Guide" | "[Name], your savings breakdown" |
| 2 | Day 0 Call Script | Permission-based | Curiosity hook |
| 3 | Day 6 Timing | Tues 10-11am | Wed 2-3pm |
| 4 | Break-Up Script | Negative reverse | Direct final offer |

**Process:**
1. Split leads 50/50
2. Run for 1 week minimum
3. Compare conversion rates
4. Adopt winner, test next element
