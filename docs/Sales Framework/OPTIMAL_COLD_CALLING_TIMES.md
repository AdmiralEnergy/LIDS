## Executive Summary

Cold calling success is heavily influenced by *when* you call, not just *how* you call. Research shows that calling at optimal times can improve connect rates by 2-3x compared to random timing. This guide provides data-backed recommendations for maximizing contact rates while staying compliant with federal and state regulations.

**Key Finding:** The best cold calling window is **Tuesday through Thursday, 10:00 AM - 11:00 AM and 4:00 PM - 5:00 PM local time** for residential solar prospects.

## The Psychology Behind Timing

### Why 10-11 AM Works Best

1. **Circadian Rhythm Alignment**
   - Cortisol (alertness hormone) peaks around 9-10 AM
   - Cognitive function is highest mid-morning for most adults
   - Prospects are awake, alert, and capable of processing new information

2. **Schedule Psychology**
   - Morning meetings typically end by 10 AM
   - Lunch meetings don't start until 11:30-12:00
   - Creates a natural "open window" in most schedules

3. **Decision Fatigue Hasn't Set In**
   - People make better decisions earlier in the day
   - By afternoon, willpower and decision capacity are depleted
   - Morning = more likely to say "yes" to a consultation

### Why 4-5 PM Is the Second-Best Window

1. **Task Completion Mindset**
   - People wrapping up work feel a sense of accomplishment
   - More open to considering new things as they transition modes

2. **Home Context**
   - If calling cell phones, homeowners are often commuting or arriving home
   - Home environment triggers thoughts about home improvements

3. **Spouse Availability**
   - Both decision-makers more likely to be reachable or together
   - Can potentially schedule follow-up when both are present

### Why Evening Calls Damage Your Brand

Even though 7-9 PM is legally permitted, research shows:

- **71% of consumers** consider evening sales calls "intrusive"
- **Complaint rates** are 3x higher for calls after 7 PM
- **Conversion rates** drop by 50%+ even when you connect
- **Caller ID reputation** degrades faster with evening calls (more blocks/reports)

The short-term gain of reaching someone at dinner is offset by long-term reputation damage. A prospect who feels intruded upon won't refer friends‚Äîthey'll warn them.

## Timezone Management

When calling NC residents from any location, always calculate **recipient's local time**.

### Quick Reference (Calling NC from Other Timezones)

| Your Location | NC Time Offset | If it's 10 AM for you, it's... |
|---------------|----------------|-------------------------------|
| Eastern (same as NC) | 0 | 10 AM in NC ‚úì |
| Central | +1 hour | 11 AM in NC ‚úì |
| Mountain | +2 hours | 12 PM in NC (lunch‚Äîavoid) |
| Pacific | +3 hours | 1 PM in NC (post-lunch OK) |

**Critical Rule:** Never assume your timezone is the prospect's timezone. CRM/dialer should display prospect's local time prominently.

### Daylight Saving Time Considerations

- NC observes DST (Eastern Time)
- Spring forward (March): Afternoon windows shift‚Äîadjust
- Fall back (November): Morning windows shift‚Äîadjust
- Arizona (no DST) callers must recalculate seasonally

## Seasonal Adjustments

### Summer (June - August)
- **Best times shift slightly later** (people stay up later, wake up later)
- **4-6 PM excellent** (long daylight hours, people outdoors but reachable)
- **Storm season messaging** works well (grid anxiety)
- **Vacation schedules** mean more callbacks, fewer first-attempt connects

### Fall (September - November)
- **Return to routine** = more predictable schedules
- **10-11 AM optimal** returns to peak
- **Back-to-school** means parents home mid-morning
- **End of year** urgency for tax credit utilization kicks in

### Winter (December - February)
- **Holiday disruption** (mid-Nov through New Year)
- **January "fresh start"** = high interest in home improvements
- **Weather events** = grid anxiety messaging opportunities
- **Shorter days** = earlier evening cutoff (people eat dinner earlier)

### Spring (March - May)
- **Tax season** (April 15) = financial decisions on hold, then released
- **Post-tax refund** = money available for down payments
- **Home improvement season** begins = high receptivity
- **DST transition** = recalculate all times in March

## Call Timing by Lead Temperature

Not all leads should be called at the same times.

### Speed-to-Lead (Hot Inbound)
- **Optimal:** Within 5-10 minutes of form submission
- **Acceptable:** Within 1 hour
- **Degraded:** After 24 hours (connect rate drops 10x)
- **Ignore time-of-day rules** for hot leads‚Äîif they just submitted at 8 PM, call them

### Warm Leads (Downloaded content, attended webinar)
- **Call within 24-48 hours** at optimal windows
- **Reference the content** they engaged with
- **Best slot:** Next available Power Hour window

### Cold List (Purchased/Generated Lists)
- **Only call during optimal windows**‚Äîno exceptions
- **Tuesday-Thursday 10-11 AM** for highest connect rates
- **Rotate through list** during prime times only

### Callbacks (Scheduled Follow-ups)
- **Honor the scheduled time exactly**
- **If no specific time given:** Default to same time you previously connected
- **"Call me back next week":** Call Tuesday 10 AM of following week

## Summary

**Optimal cold calling times exist because human behavior is predictable.** People are most receptive when they're alert but not busy, settled but not distracted, and in a mindset conducive to considering home improvements.

For Admiral Energy's NC residential solar sales:

1. **Primary Focus:** Tuesday-Thursday, 10-11:30 AM and 4-5:30 PM
2. **Secondary:** Monday/Friday mid-morning only
3. **Avoid:** Before 9:30 AM, during lunch, after 6:30 PM, Sundays
4. **Never:** Violate TCPA/state time restrictions
